Read this before you walk in.

Four tier prices. Four scripts. Four pushback answers. One walk-away line. Memorize the walk-away.

Three rules before you start.

  • Lead with Tier 1 · Foundation · $3,000. That's the recommended default. Do not open with $28K.
  • Make Scott name his budget first. Don't quote a number until he does.
  • When he pushes back, the answer is "No, the prices are the work." Don't discount. Don't apologize.
Part 1

The four tier talk tracks.

Tier 0 · Card · $1,000

One page. 2–3 days.

One page. Your name, phone, address, hours, services, one review, a map. Built in 2 to 3 days. It's a digital business card — it gets you found when someone Googles "Jaynes Flooring" by name. It will not bring you new customers who don't already know your name. This is the absolute minimum to exist online.
Tier 1 · Foundation · $3,000Start here

Five pages. One week.

Five real pages — home, services, about, reviews, contact. Works on every phone. Google Business Profile set up so you show up on Google Maps. A quote-request form that emails Sydnee the second someone fills it in. Live in a week. This is the starting line — when somebody searches "flooring near me" in Greene County, you actually appear. Every other flooring store in the Tri-Cities is at this level. This is where you stop being invisible.
Tier 2 · Growth · $8,000–$12,000

Catch up. Pass them.

Everything in Foundation, plus a team page, a before-and-after gallery, the brands you carry, an upgraded lead form, Facebook and Instagram business accounts set up properly, and you start showing up in ChatGPT and Claude when people ask them "best flooring store East Tennessee." Two weeks to build. This catches you up to Appalachian Flooring and puts you past Dockery Abbey.
Tier 3 · Signature · $28,000

Dominate. 4–6 weeks.

Everything in Growth, plus a custom sample-ordering app where customers pick five samples online and you ship them — every order is a named lead with their address. A 20-second branded commercial cut for TV, Instagram, Facebook, and TikTok. TikTok account added. The 11-page Market Intelligence Report. Live training session for Sydnee. Four to six weeks. This is the level that makes Jaynes the digital leader in East Tennessee before Floor & Decor shows up.
Part 2

When he pushes back.

"I can't pay that much."

→ "What's the budget you're working with?" Make him name a number first. Then match a tier to it.

"Can you do the Signature for $5,000?"

→ "No. The Signature has four to six weeks of custom software work in it. What I can do is start you on Foundation for $3,000 — that gets you properly visible on Google, which is the most important thing — and you upgrade later by paying the difference."

"My nephew can do it for $500."

→ "Good — he'll learn a lot. The $500 site won't appear on Google and won't bring you customers. When it doesn't work out, I'm still here."

"Can you discount?"

→ "No. The prices are the work."

Part 3

The walk-away.

Say this out loud. Mean it.

"I built four real options at four real prices. Pick the one that fits, or pick none. I'd rather you walk away than I undercut what I built."

Then stop talking. Whoever speaks next loses.

You already did the work. Now go close it.
