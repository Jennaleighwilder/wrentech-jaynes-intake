Read this before you walk in.

Scott is a good man who built something real. This is how to help him see what we built, without pressuring him.

Remember who you're sitting across from.

Scott built Jaynes Flooring with handshakes. He's kind. He's earned every bit of his reputation. And he has no real frame of reference for what any of this costs — because nobody in his world has ever needed to know.

Our job today isn't to sell him. Our job is to gently show him what's true: the market has moved, the people who buy floors are looking online now, and we built him something honest he can use. He gets to decide what fits.

Three things to keep in mind.

  • Open at Tier 1 · Foundation · $3,000. Frame it as the right-sized step, not the cheap one.
  • Ask what he's comfortable spending before you put a price on the table. Let him lead.
  • If a number doesn't feel right to him, that's okay. We're not here to convince him into anything — we're here to give him good information.
Part 1

The four tier talk tracks.

Tier 0 · Card · $1,000

One page. 2–3 days.

This one is just so Jaynes exists on the internet — one clean page with your name, phone, address, hours, services, a customer review, and a map. Done in two or three days. Honestly, Scott, if budget is tight this is a fine place to start. It won't bring in many new customers, but it makes sure anyone who hears your name can find you.
Tier 1 · Foundation · $3,000This is the one we'd suggest

Five pages. Live in a week.

This is the one we think fits Jaynes best. Five real pages — home, services, about, reviews, contact. Works on every phone. We set up your Google Business Profile so you show up on Google Maps. There's a quote form so when someone fills it out, it goes straight to Sydnee's inbox. Live in about a week. This is what every other flooring store in the Tri-Cities already has — it's how you stop being invisible online, without making this a huge investment.
Tier 2 · Growth · $8,000–$12,000

If you want to catch up and pass them.

If down the road you want more reach, this adds a team page, a before-and-after gallery, the brands you carry, a stronger lead form, and proper Facebook and Instagram pages set up so the wives and daughters doing the searching actually find you. Two weeks to build. This is where you start passing Appalachian and Dockery on Google. We're not pushing you toward this — it's just here if and when you're ready.
Tier 3 · Signature · $28,000

The full build. 4–6 weeks.

This is the whole vision — sample-ordering software where customers pick five samples from your site and you ship them, a short branded video for TV and social, TikTok added, the full market research report, and a training session for Sydnee. Four to six weeks. We built it out so you could see what the top end looks like, but we're not expecting you to start here. It's the long-game version if it ever makes sense.
Part 2

If he hesitates.

"That's more than I was thinking."

→ "Totally understand. What were you picturing in your head? Let's start there and see what fits."

"Can you do the bigger one for less?"

→ "I wish we could, Scott. The Signature has four to six weeks of work in it, including custom software, so we can't move on that price without cutting the work itself. The honest answer is the $3,000 Foundation will do the most important job — getting you found on Google — and you can always add the rest later by paying the difference."

"My grandson said he could build me something."

→ "That's wonderful — let him. If it works out, you've saved money and helped him learn. If you ever want to revisit, we're not going anywhere. We just want what's best for the business."

"Is there any wiggle room?"

→ "On the work, no — the prices reflect what's actually in each tier. But we can absolutely shape the scope to whatever number works for you. Tell us your number and we'll show you what fits inside it."

Part 3

If he's not ready.

Say this kindly. Mean it.

"Scott, there's no pressure here. We built this because we believe in what you've built — and we wanted you to have honest options. Take it home. Talk to Faye and Sydnee. We'll be here whenever you want to talk again, whether that's next week or next year."

Then let the room breathe. Don't fill the silence. He'll either say a number, ask a question, or thank you — any of those is a good outcome.

You already did the work. The rest is just being honest with a good man.
